### Lesson Summary
To recap, **information security** is a collection of rules or processes that protect information. A **threat** is anything that can negatively affect something of interest. And thus, a **threat to information security** is anything that can negatively affect information. Threats can be internal or external, physical or not. Either way, information security must recognize, and protect against, them all. This can be a huge undertaking when you consider the number of possible threats.
