### Lesson Summary
From ancient times when people looked for creative ways to hide information, to advanced modern cryptography, the field of **information security** has changed along with technology and the nature of information itself. Simple substitution ciphers used by kings and generals evolved into ever more complex schemes as adversaries learned each others' tactics. Mechanical rotor machines like the German Enigma took encryption beyond what people could compute by hand. Finally, as the world became digitized, complex computerized encryption from DES to RSA and PKI revolutionized how we protect vital information. The future of InfoSec may lie in the cutting-edge field of quantum computation.

### Quiz
1. What is the 'key exchange' problem in modern information security?
   * Two parties need to privately share the secret encryption key before communicating.
2. You have just intercepted the following message from Caesar: L ORYH FOHRSDWUD. Can you guess what shift was used to encode the message?
   * 3 = I LOVE CLEOPATRA
3. Encrypt the following message using a Caesar cipher with a shift of 7: ET TU BRUTE
   * LA AB IYBAL
4. What is a one-way function in cryptography?
   * A mathematical combination of keys that is easy to create, but difficult to undo.
5. What was the name of the cryptographic rotor machine used by the Germans in World War II?
   * The Enigma machine
