### Lesson Summary
**Cybercrime** refers to the use of a computer to commit a crime. As a criminal activity, it began when hackers started illegally accessing high-level computer networks. Some examples of cybercrime include credit card and identity theft, network intrusions and software piracy.

Federal laws associated with the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA), like **18 U.S.C. Section 1030**, make it a crime to attack computer systems, an activity that can lead to a jail sentence. Some states, like New York, also have trespassing laws that pertain specifically to computers.
